## Purpose

`Research` is the inquiry and incubation layer of the vault.

It holds developing questions, uncertain synthesis, literature exploration, professional research thinking, and exploratory intellectual material not yet stable enough for `Atlas`.

## Inheritance

This README inherits the root vault contract in `[[README]]`.

It carries over `[[README#Global conventions]]` and `[[README#README rules]]`.

The sections below add local rules for this directory.

If a local rule appears to conflict with a parent rule, flag it for human review.

## What belongs here

- Developing research questions.
- Uncertain synthesis and exploratory arguments.
- Literature exploration and reading notes.
- Professional research thinking.
- External collaborator code, results, and proposal context as source mapping and interpretation, while active execution belongs in `Efforts`.
- Ideas that may later become `Efforts`, `Atlas`, or `Worlds`.
- Sensitive or speculative inquiry that needs careful framing.

## What does not belong here

- Stable concepts: use [[Atlas/README]].
- Active executable work: use [[Efforts/README]].
- Dated traces, meetings, and logs: use [[Timeline/README]].
- Heavy PDFs, datasets, binaries, raw evidence, and media: use [[Supplementary/README]].
- Public-facing narratives: use [[Worlds/README]].

## Important entry points

## Relationship to nearby directories

| Directory | Relationship |
| --------- | ------------ |
| `Atlas` | Receives stable concepts and durable references; stable people anchors and self-model anchors belong in Atlas. |
| `Efforts` | Receives research that becomes executable work. |
| `Timeline` | Holds dated meetings, logs, and traces related to research. |
| `Supplementary` | Stores heavy research evidence and files linked from notes. |
| `Worlds` | Receives public-facing narratives after research matures. |

## Current state

`Research` may contain mixed levels of certainty and maturity.

Some notes may later move conceptually toward `Atlas`, `Efforts`, or `Worlds`.

## Local conventions

- Mark uncertainty clearly.
- Prefer source-aware claims over confident synthesis.
- Use links to evidence instead of storing heavy files here.
- Keep speculative, personal, spiritual, or sensitive inquiry nuanced.
- Isolate the direction and the question before automating collection or turning the idea into an effort.
- Suggest destinations before moving anything.

## Known issues

- Uncertain notes can look more stable than they are.
- Research can become active work without being moved to `Efforts`.
- Stable concepts may need later distillation into `Atlas`.
